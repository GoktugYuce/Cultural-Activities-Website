St. Mark's Square

Piazza San Marco, often known in English as St Mark's Square, is the principal public square of Venice, Italy, where it is generally known just as la Piazza. Almost all the other urban spaces in the city are called campi. The Piazzetta is an extension of the Piazza towards San Marco basin in its southeast corner.

schedule

step 1

Approach the square on foot, emerging from the narrow streets, or arrive in style by gondola or water taxi. Take a moment to absorb the sight: the stunning St. Mark's Basilica, the imposing Doge's Palace, the elegant clock tower, and flocks of pigeons.

step 2

Dedicate time to exploring this Byzantine masterpiece. Admire the golden mosaics on its facade, and if time allows (and you've pre-booked tickets), step inside to experience the opulent interior. Tour the former center of Venetian political power with its grand halls, hidden passages, and the Bridge of Sighs (refer to the separate Doge's Palace plan for ticketing).

step 3

Sit down at one of the historic cafes lining the square (like Caffè Florian or Caffè Quadri). Yes, they're pricey, but it's a quintessential Venetian experience. Grab a spot on a bench and people-watch the square is a stage for locals, tourists, and costumed performers.
