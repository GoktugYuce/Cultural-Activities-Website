pantheon

The Pantheon is a former Roman temple and, since AD 609, a Catholic church in Rome, Italy. It was built on the site of an earlier temple commissioned by Marcus Agrippa during the reign of Augustus, then after that burnt down, the present building was ordered by the emperor Hadrian and probably dedicated c. AD 126.

schedule

step 1

The Pantheon is located in the heart of Rome's historic center, within the bustling Piazza della Rotonda. You can easily walk to the Pantheon from many central locations or take public transportation (buses stop nearby). Be prepared for crowds, especially during peak season.

step 2

Before entering, take a moment to admire the Pantheon's facade the massive portico with its towering Corinthian columns and triangular pediment. Note the ancient inscription dedicating the building. Entering the Pantheon: Step inside and be amazed by the sheer scale of the interior. Its vast dome and the famous oculus (circular opening at the top) create a breathtaking sense of space and light.

step 3

Take some time to simply soak in the atmosphere of this remarkable ancient structure with its unique design. Consider renting an audioguide for deeper historical insights if desired.
