David of Michelangelo

David is a masterpiece of Italian Renaissance sculpture, created from 1501 to 1504 by Michelangelo. With a height of 5.17 metres, the David was the first colossal marble statue made in the early modern period following classical antiquity, a precedent for the 16th century and beyond.

schedule

step 1

Aim to arrive at the gallery a bit before your designated entry time to allow for security and checking your belongings if needed.

step 2

Follow signs within the Accademia Gallery. David is prominently displayed with ample space around him for viewing. Take some time to walk fully around the statue, appreciating its imposing beauty from various angles. Notice how the details and sense of tension shift with your viewpoint.

step 3

The Accademia Gallery houses other unfinished sculptures by Michelangelo, showcasing his process and offering a broader perspective on his genius. If time allows, explore the rest of the gallery, which showcases Florentine paintings and other sculptures.
