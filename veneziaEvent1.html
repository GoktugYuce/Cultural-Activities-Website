Doge's Palace

The Doge's Palace is a palace built in Venetian Gothic style, and one of the main landmarks of the city of Venice in northern Italy. The palace was the residence of the Doge of Venice, the supreme authority of the former Republic of Venice. It was built in 1340 and extended and modified in the following centuries.

schedule

step 1

Doge's Palace dominates one side of the bustling Piazza San Marco, the heart of Venice. Arrive on foot or via a scenic vaporetto (water bus) ride. If interested in the Secret Itineraries Tour (including hidden passages and prison cells), pre-booking for limited spots is essential.

step 2

Enter through the designated gate and marvel at the interior courtyard. Ascend the opulent staircase decorated with gold and stucco.

step 3

Allocate time to wander the halls and chambers at your own pace, soaking in the lavish details. If interested, explore the Palace's collection of weapons and armor. If short on time, focus on the key highlights. For deeper immersion, allow ample time or choose a more specialized tour.
