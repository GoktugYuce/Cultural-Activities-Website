Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore

Florence Cathedral, formally the Cathedral of Saint Mary of the Flower, is the cathedral of Florence, Italy. It was begun in 1296 in the Gothic style to a design of Arnolfo di Cambio and was structurally completed by 1436, with the dome engineered by Filippo Brunelleschi.

schedule

step 1

The Duomo dominates the heart of Florence's Piazza del Duomo. Make your way on foot or via public transportation. Take a moment to soak in the impressive scale and beauty of the cathedral's multi-colored marble facade and the iconic dome towering above.

step 2

Enter the main cathedral (entry is free) to admire its vast interior, stained glass windows, and frescoes (note areas may be closed off depending on times/services). If time allows and you're up for a challenge, purchase tickets in advance to climb Brunelleschi's Dome for breathtaking panoramic views of Florence.

step 3

Depending on what you choose, allocate enough time for each chosen area so you don't feel rushed. Allow time to simply wander the cathedral interior or around the Piazza del Duomo taking photos and soaking in the lively atmosphere.
